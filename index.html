Cinematography language

What makes a prompt cinematic?

A cinematic prompt describes how the scene is captured, not just what is in it. These six elements are what PromptKits uses to transform a basic idea into a professional prompt.

📐 Shot type
Wide, medium, close-up, bird's eye, low angle — each creates a different emotional effect on the viewer.
🔭 Lens choice
24mm exaggerates depth, 85mm flatters faces, anamorphic creates the oval bokeh and lens flares of cinema.
💡 Lighting direction
Light from the side creates depth and dimension. Golden hour, Rembrandt, split, soft window — each tells a different story.
🎨 Colour grade
Teal and orange for Hollywood, cold desaturated for thrillers, warm vintage for period drama — grade signals genre.
🎥 Camera movement
Dolly push-in creates intimacy, crane rising creates revelation, handheld creates documentary urgency.
🌫 Atmosphere
Morning mist, rain reflections, dust in light — atmosphere transforms an ordinary scene into a cinematic one.
💡 VFX pro tip
Lighting direction is the single most impactful element in any AI image prompt. Always specify it first — before subject, before style, before anything else.
🎬 Video tip
Always include camera movement in video prompts. "Slow dolly push-in" produces far better results than describing only the subject.
