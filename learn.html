🔭 Lens Guide

How focal length changes your AI video — perspective, compression, and depth of field explained

Ultra-Wide

14–20mm Ultra-Wide

What it does

Extreme field of view that bends straight lines and exaggerates spatial depth. Objects close to the lens appear massive while the background recedes dramatically.

Best for

Establishing shots, interior environments, landscape scale, dramatic architecture, first-person perspective scenes.

Avoid

Portraits — facial distortion at the edges makes subjects look unnatural.

Sample prompt
"Ultra-wide 14mm establishing shot, massive foreground rock formation dwarfing the canyon below, extreme depth of field, morning mist in the valley, cinematic anamorphic quality"
Wide

24–35mm Wide

What it does

Slightly wider than human vision. Shows the subject within their environment without extreme distortion. The standard lens for photojournalism and documentary work.

Best for

Environmental portraits, street scenes, interior shots, handheld documentary sequences, travel filmmaking.

Avoid

Close-up portraits — slight barrel distortion at close range widens facial features.

Sample prompt
"35mm wide lens, street market at dusk, subject in foreground buying fruit, the busy market life continuing behind, warm practical light from stalls, natural depth"
Standard

50mm Standard

What it does

Closest to the natural field of human vision. Renders scenes with no distortion, no compression — exactly as the eye sees them. The most honest lens.

Best for

Conversational dialogue scenes, candid portraits, food photography, neutral establishing shots, everyday realism.

Avoid

Dramatic impact shots — the neutral perspective gives no visual exaggeration.

Sample prompt
"50mm standard lens, two people at a cafe table, natural window light, the conversation feeling intimate but observed, no distortion, warm ambient light"
Portrait

85mm Portrait

What it does

The classic portrait focal length. Renders faces with natural proportions while creating beautiful background separation. The most flattering lens for human subjects.

Best for

Portrait close-ups, interview shots, character moments, beauty shots, any scene requiring subject isolation.

Avoid

Wide scenes — the longer focal length compresses space and excludes environmental context.

Sample prompt
"85mm portrait lens, subject lit by soft window light, f1.8 shallow depth of field, creamy bokeh background, face rendered with perfect proportions, cinematic close-up"
Telephoto

135–200mm Telephoto

What it does

Compresses distance between foreground and background, stacking the scene into a flattened plane. Creates the illusion that everything is close together.

Best for

Wildlife, sports, candid street photography, subjects in crowds, compression storytelling, paparazzi-style observational shots.

Avoid

Interior scenes — too much compression in confined spaces flattens depth completely.

Sample prompt
"200mm telephoto lens, lone figure walking through a crowded city street, the crowd compressed tightly behind, faces stacked, the subject isolated within the mass"
Macro

Macro

What it does

Achieves 1:1 or greater magnification, revealing details invisible to the naked eye. The world at macro scale is alien, textural, and geometrically extraordinary.

Best for

Product detail, insect and nature close-ups, food texture, mechanical detail, science and biology content.

Avoid

Any moving subject — depth of field at macro scale is measured in millimetres.

Sample prompt
"Macro lens extreme close-up, dewdrop on a spider web strand, perfect sphere reflection of the forest inside the drop, shallow depth of field, morning light"

🎨 Color & Tone Guide

The colour grades that define cinematic genres — and the exact prompt language to achieve each one

Cinematic

Teal & Orange

Epic · Heroic · High-energy
What it looks like

The most used colour grade in Hollywood. Warm skin tones (orange) pop against cooled shadows and backgrounds (teal). Complementary colours that make faces glow.

Best for

Action films, blockbusters, hero shots, any scene needing high-energy visual impact.

Sample prompt
"Teal and orange colour grade, warm skin tones glowing in afternoon light, cooled blue-green shadows in the background, cinematic blockbuster look"
Warm

Golden Hour Warm

Nostalgic · Romantic · Beautiful
What it looks like

Pushes the entire palette toward amber and gold. Shadows lift to warm brown, highlights bloom to cream. The light of nostalgia, safety, and beauty.

Best for

Romance, nostalgia, summer scenes, beauty content, lifestyle brands.

Sample prompt
"Golden hour colour grade, amber highlights, warm brown shadows, the entire frame bathed in honey-toned light, film grain, soft bloom on highlights"
Muted

Desaturated Muted

Realistic · Gritty · Serious
What it looks like

Pulls the saturation out of the entire image, leaving colours present but drained. The visual language of realism, exhaustion, and moral complexity.

Best for

Drama, war films, documentary realism, character studies, grief narratives.

Sample prompt
"Desaturated colour grade, muted palette, colours present but drained of vibrancy, cold grey-green tones, harsh overhead light, documentary realism"
Cold

Cold Blue Steel

Cold · Tense · Technological
What it looks like

Pushes shadows and midtones into blue-grey steel. Clinical, cold, detached. The colour of technology, danger, and psychological distance.

Best for

Sci-fi, thriller, corporate dystopia, night scenes, anything requiring emotional coldness.

Sample prompt
"Cold blue-steel colour grade, deep blue shadows, clinical midtones, the scene stripped of warmth, night interior with fluorescent practical lights, thriller atmosphere"
B&W

High Contrast Black & White

Timeless · Dramatic · Artistic
What it looks like

Pure luminance storytelling. Removes colour entirely, forcing the viewer to read light, shadow, texture, and form. The most timeless and most graphic look.

Best for

Classic drama, historical content, fine art, high-contrast character studies, noir.

Sample prompt
"High contrast black and white, deep blacks, bright whites, dramatic shadow play, the texture of skin and fabric fully rendered, no colour, pure form"
Bleach

Bleach Bypass

Dread · Harsh · Visceral
What it looks like

Silver retention look — high contrast, reduced saturation, crushed blacks with retained grain. Feels expensive and harsh simultaneously. The look of war films and horror.

Best for

Horror, war, survival narratives, psychological thriller, any scene needing dread.

Sample prompt
"Bleach bypass colour grade, silver retention look, high contrast, muted colours, crushed blacks, aggressive grain, the dread of the look itself"
Neon

Neon Cyberpunk

Futuristic · Alienated · Vivid
What it looks like

Deep crushed blacks with saturated practical light sources in magenta, cyan, and green. The visual grammar of technological alienation and nocturnal urban life.

Best for

Sci-fi, cyberpunk, night club scenes, futuristic environments, music videos.

Sample prompt
"Cyberpunk neon colour grade, deep black environment, saturated magenta and cyan practical lights, the darkness as canvas for the neon to paint on, urban night"
Vintage

Vintage Film

Nostalgic · Warm · Imperfect
What it looks like

Lifted blacks that never go pure black, faded highlights, colour shifts toward yellow-green in the shadows. The specific imperfection of photochemical film emulsion.

Best for

Period pieces, nostalgia, retro content, music videos with vintage aesthetic.

Sample prompt
"Vintage film emulsion colour grade, lifted black point, slightly faded highlights, shadow colour shift toward yellow-green, 1970s film look, grain"

📐 Camera Angle Guide

Every angle communicates a specific emotional relationship between viewer and subject

Top-Down

Bird's Eye / Top Down

Reveals system · Creates abstraction · Shows pattern
What it communicates

Directly overhead, looking straight down. Reveals geometry, pattern and system invisible from ground level. The most abstract and design-conscious camera angle.

Best for

Establishing urban scale, revealing crowd patterns, food photography, map-like spatial context.

Sample prompt
"Bird's eye overhead shot looking straight down, the city grid as pure geometry, pedestrians as moving dots, the urban system revealed as designed pattern"
Worm's Eye

Worm's Eye / Low Angle

Dominance · Power · Imposing scale
What it communicates

Camera at or below ground level, shooting upward. Makes subjects tower above the viewer. Communicates power, dominance, and imposing scale.

Best for

Establishing villain power, architectural grandeur, heroic moments, intimidating characters.

Sample prompt
"Worm's eye low angle looking up, the skyscraper towers into the sky, the human figure at the base dwarfed by the architecture, dramatic foreshortening"
Eye Level

Eye Level

Equality · Neutrality · Direct connection
What it communicates

The camera at the subject's eye height. Creates equality between viewer and subject — neither dominant nor submissive. The most natural and neutral angle.

Best for

Dialogue scenes, character introduction, documentary interviews, balanced emotional scenes.

Sample prompt
"Eye level camera, face to face with the subject, neither looking up nor down, the most direct and honest relationship between viewer and character"
Dutch Angle

Dutch Angle / Tilt

Unease · Disorientation · Psychological disturbance
What it communicates

Camera tilted on the roll axis so the horizon is not level. Communicates psychological instability, unease, and disorientation. Used sparingly for maximum effect.

Best for

Psychological horror, villain scenes, drug sequences, moral disorientation, unreliable narrator.

Sample prompt
"Dutch angle tilted frame, the room at 20 degrees, the character's world literally tilted, psychological unease visible in the camera itself"
High Angle

High Angle

Vulnerability · Observation · Smallness
What it communicates

Camera above eye level, looking down on the subject. Makes subjects appear smaller, more vulnerable, or more observed. The surveillance angle.

Best for

Showing character vulnerability, surveillance POV, crowd scenes, establishing geography.

Sample prompt
"High angle looking down on the character in the empty plaza, their smallness within the vast space communicating isolation and vulnerability"
POV

POV / First Person

Immersion · Identity merger · Maximum tension
What it communicates

Camera placed exactly where a character's eyes would be. The viewer becomes the character. Maximum immersion, zero observer distance.

Best for

Action sequences, horror first-person horror, immersive storytelling, video game aesthetics.

Sample prompt
"First person POV, the hands reaching through the crowd, the camera at eye height, the world experienced rather than observed, handheld urgency"
OTS

Over the Shoulder

Relationship · Context · Conversational dynamic
What it communicates

Camera positioned behind and slightly to one side of a character's shoulder, looking past them at another character. The standard dialogue coverage setup.

Best for

Dialogue scenes, confrontations, interview scenarios, establishing character relationships.

Sample prompt
"Over the shoulder shot, the character in the foreground blurred, the other person in full focus beyond them, the space between them charged with the conversation"
Aerial

Aerial / Drone

Scale · Context · Discovery
What it communicates

Camera elevated above the scene, typically by drone or crane. Reveals landscape scale, environmental context, and spatial relationships impossible from ground level.

Best for

Location establishing shots, travel content, scale communication, landscape cinematography.

Sample prompt
"Aerial drone shot ascending from the village to reveal the mountain range beyond, the human settlement suddenly tiny against geological scale"

💡 Lighting Guide

Light is the primary language of cinema — understand each setup before writing it into your prompt

Natural

Golden Hour

2000–3500K · Warm gold
What it looks like

The hour after sunrise and before sunset when the sun is low, light is warm (2000-3500K), and shadows are long. The most sought-after natural light in photography and film.

Best for

Outdoor portraits, landscape cinematography, romantic scenes, product lifestyle shots.

Sample prompt
"Golden hour, sun low on the horizon behind the subject, warm rim light separating them from the background, long shadows stretching forward, the world glowing amber"
Soft

Overcast / Soft Box

6000–7000K · Cool white
What it looks like

Clouds act as a giant natural diffuser, wrapping light around subjects from all directions. Shadows are soft with no hard edges. The most flattering light for faces.

Best for

Portrait photography, beauty shots, product photography requiring no harsh shadows.

Sample prompt
"Overcast soft light, no directional shadow, the subject fully wrapped in diffused illumination, skin textures visible without harsh shadow, gentle and honest light"
Studio

Rembrandt Lighting

3200–5600K · Tungsten to daylight
What it looks like

Key light at 45° above and 45° to the side, creating a triangle of light on the shadow cheek. Named after the Dutch master who made it the definitive portrait lighting technique.

Best for

Dramatic portraits, character study, classical fine art photography, cinematic close-ups.

Sample prompt
"Rembrandt lighting, the triangle of light on the shadow side cheek, deep shadow on the far side of the face, single warm key light from above-left, classical portrait"
Backlight

Backlight / Rim Light

Any temperature · Effect over warmth
What it looks like

Light source placed behind the subject, creating a glowing edge that separates them from the background. Creates visual depth and a halo effect that communicates significance.

Best for

Hero shots, product photography, dramatic reveals, spiritual or powerful character moments.

Sample prompt
"Backlit rim light, the subject glowing at their edges, the background darker, a separation between figure and environment that reads as significance"
Practical

Practical Lighting

1800–4000K · Warm to neutral
What it looks like

Using the actual lights visible in the scene — lamps, candles, neon signs, monitors — as the primary or only light source. Creates authentic atmosphere and motivated light.

Best for

Interior scenes, moody atmospheres, night scenes, period drama, documentary realism.

Sample prompt
"Practical lighting only, the scene lit by the lamp on the desk and the glow of the computer screen, no added light, the warmth and shadow of real interior life"
Hard

Hard Light / Single Source

5600K · Daylight balanced
What it looks like

A small, undiffused light source creating hard-edged shadows with maximum contrast. Unforgiving and dramatic — every texture is exaggerated, every surface exposed.

Best for

Villain scenes, interrogation rooms, horror, war photography, intense character moments.

Sample prompt
"Single hard light source from above-left, harsh shadows with clean edges, maximum contrast on the face, the light as an interrogator not a flatterer"
Dusk

Blue Hour / Dusk

10000K sky vs 2700K windows · Contrast
What it looks like

The 20-minute window after sunset when the sky is deep blue and artificial lights begin to compete. The specific quality where cold ambient and warm practical exist simultaneously.

Best for

Urban cinematography, transition scenes, romantic evening atmosphere, city establishing shots.

Sample prompt
"Blue hour, the sky deep blue behind the city skyline, warm yellow windows glowing in the buildings, the specific light where the day ends and the night begins"
Low Key

Low Key / Shadow

Any · Quantity controlled, not temperature
What it looks like

Predominantly dark image with the subject partially or mostly in shadow. Light is sculpted rather than flooded — revealing only what the photographer chooses to show.

Best for

Mystery, thriller, horror, villains, psychological depth, film noir aesthetics.

Sample prompt
"Low key lighting, deep shadows dominating the frame, the subject partially revealed in a single pool of light, the darkness as active as the light"
