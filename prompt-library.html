What makes these prompts different?

Most AI prompt libraries are just lists of keywords. These prompts are structured descriptions that communicate the visual language of professional filmmaking to AI models.

Every prompt specifies the elements that actually make a shot cinematic:

  • Camera movement — dolly push-in, crane rising, tracking, orbital, handheld
  • Lens choice — 24mm wide, 85mm portrait, anamorphic, telephoto compression
  • Lighting direction — golden hour backlight, Rembrandt, split, soft window, neon practical
  • Colour grade — teal and orange, cold desaturated, warm vintage, natural
  • Atmosphere — morning mist, rain reflections, dust in light, volumetric haze

Frequently asked questions

Are all the prompts really free?
Yes — every prompt in every collection is completely free to copy and use for any purpose, commercial or personal. No account required.
Which AI tools work with these prompts?
Video prompts work with Kling AI, Runway Gen-3, Sora, and Pika. Image prompts work with Midjourney, DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion, and Leonardo AI.
Can I modify the prompts?
Absolutely. Every prompt is a template. Replace the subject description with your own content while keeping the camera movement, lighting, and colour grade to maintain the cinematic quality.
How often are new prompts added?
New collections are added regularly. The library currently has 11 collections with 950+ prompts and grows with new topics and AI tool-specific collections.
What makes a prompt cinematic?
A cinematic prompt specifies how the scene is captured — the camera movement, lens choice, lighting direction, and colour grade. These technical elements are what separate professional-looking AI output from generic results.
Where can I learn how to write my own prompts?
The Learning Hub has 17 free lessons covering everything from basic prompt structure to advanced cinematic techniques, camera movements, and scene consistency.
