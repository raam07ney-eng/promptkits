What is Image-to-Prompt Reverse Engineering?

Image-to-prompt reverse engineering is the process of analysing an existing image and generating the text prompt that would recreate it — or produce something with a similar visual style, composition, and aesthetic. It is an essential skill for AI creators who want to understand why certain images look the way they do and how to reproduce that quality deliberately.

The PromptKits Image to Prompt tool uses AI vision technology to analyse uploaded images across multiple dimensions — composition, lighting, colour palette, style, texture, mood, and technical qualities — and converts that analysis into actionable prompts for Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, and other image generation tools.

This is particularly valuable when you encounter an AI-generated image whose style you want to replicate, when you have a reference photo that captures the visual mood you want, or when you want to understand what specific prompt language produces particular visual effects.

What the AI Analyses in Your Image

Composition & Framing

The rule of thirds placement, the relationship between foreground and background, depth of field, the angle and height of the viewpoint, and the overall geometric structure of the image.

Lighting Quality & Direction

The apparent light source direction, the hardness or softness of the light, the colour temperature, the presence of shadows and highlights, and whether the light is natural or artificial.

Colour Palette & Grade

The dominant and accent colours, the overall warmth or coolness of the colour grade, saturation levels, contrast, and any distinctive colour grading styles like teal and orange or desaturated cool tones.

Style & Medium

Whether the image appears photographic or illustrative, the specific artistic style if present, the rendering quality, whether it appears to be AI-generated and in what style, and references to real-world artists or movements.

Mood & Atmosphere

The emotional register of the image, the implied narrative or story, the time of day and weather conditions, and the overall feeling that the visual elements create together.
